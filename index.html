From 1924 to 2014, Canada has achieved a lot of medals.

In total, Canada has won 199 medals at the Olympic Winter Games: 73 gold, 64 silver and 62 bronze medals. This does not include the gold medal in ice hockey won by Canada at the 1920 Olympic Games; while considered the first Olympic medal in ice hockey, it preceded the establishment of the Olympic Winter Games. The country ranks fifth in the total number of medals won at the Olympic Winter Games.

Total of Medals compare to Men and Women

Canada has competed at every Winter Olympic Games, and has won at least one medal each time. By total medals, the country's best performance was both male and female. However, men have more medals than women.

The medals win by women by year.

The rate of participation of women in the Olympics has been increasing since their first participation in 1948. Some sports are uniquely for women, others are contested by both sexes, while some older sports remain for men only. Studies of media coverage of the Olympics consistently show differences in the ways in which women and men are described and the ways in which their performances are discussed.

The comparison between women and men.

Looking at pie charts, in 1948, the number of medals won by women is less than men. While in 2010, the number of medals won by women increase almost equal men.
